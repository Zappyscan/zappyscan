# How to Get Investors for Your Startup
## A Complete Guide — From Zero to Funded
### Written for Zappy Restaurant OS | June 2026

---

## Overview

Getting investors is a process, not an event. It takes preparation, the right narrative, targeted outreach, and a clear understanding of what investors actually look for. This guide walks you through every stage — from the day you decide to raise, to the day you sign a term sheet and close your round.

---

## STAGE 1: Before You Even Talk to Investors

### 1.1 Know What You're Building and Why It Matters

Before any investor meeting, you must be able to answer three questions in one breath:

1. **What is the problem?** (specific, with data)
2. **What is your solution?** (simple, clear)
3. **Why you, why now?** (your unfair advantage)

For Zappy, this is: "India's 7.5 million restaurants lose ₹2 lakh per month from paper-ticket chaos and no digital ops. Zappy is the real-time restaurant OS — QR ordering, kitchen sync, waiter dashboard, billing, analytics — one URL, no hardware, starting free. We built the full MVP in 6 months and it's live."

Practice saying this in 30 seconds. This is your elevator pitch.

### 1.2 Validate Before You Fundraise

Investors fund conviction, not ideas. The more validation you have, the less risk they perceive:

- **Paying customers** are the strongest signal
- **Free users with strong engagement** is the next best thing
- **Letters of intent (LOIs)** from potential customers signal demand
- **Working MVP** shows execution capability
- **Clear user feedback** shows you understand the market

At Zappy's current stage (MVP live, testing with 4 tenants), the message should be: "We have a fully built, tested, live product. We're now raising to go from product-ready to market-ready."

### 1.3 Know Your Numbers Cold

You must memorize and be able to defend:

| Metric | Your Number |
|--------|-------------|
| Funding ask | ₹2 Crore |
| Equity offered | 10-15% |
| Use of funds (breakdown) | Engineering 40%, Sales 30%, Ops 15%, WC 10%, Legal 5% |
| Burn rate | ₹8-10L/month post-funding |
| Runway | 18 months |
| Target MRR at Month 18 | ₹4.8L (200 restaurants) |
| Target ARR at Month 18 | ₹57.6L |
| Series A plan | ₹15-25Cr at Month 24 |
| TAM | ₹1,200 Crore (India restaurant tech) |
| LTV:CAC target | 15:1 |

If you hesitate on any of these in a meeting, it damages credibility immediately.

## STAGE 2: Documents You Must Prepare

Prepare every document before you start outreach. Investors will ask for these at different stages — having them ready shows you're serious and organized.

### Document Checklist

#### Tier 1 — For First Contact (share before the first meeting)

| Document | Purpose | Status |
|----------|---------|--------|
| **One-Pager** | 1-page summary of the business — problem, solution, traction, ask | Prepare this first |
| **Pitch Deck (PPT)** | 10-20 slide visual story for investor meetings | ✅ Done |
| **Executive Summary** | 2-page PDF version of your pitch | Include in PDF report |
| **Live Product Link** | Link to working product investors can explore | ✅ zappy-blond.vercel.app |

#### Tier 2 — For Serious Interest (share after first positive meeting)

| Document | Purpose | Status |
|----------|---------|--------|
| **Detailed Project Report (PDF)** | Comprehensive written report covering all aspects | ✅ Done |
| **Financial Model (Excel)** | 3-year P&L, MRR projections, use of funds breakdown | Prepare this |
| **Cap Table** | Current ownership structure, pre and post-money | Prepare this |
| **Product Demo Video** | 3-5 minute screen-recorded walkthrough of the product | Record this |
| **Customer Testimonials / LOIs** | Letters of intent or quotes from interested restaurants | Collect these |

#### Tier 3 — For Due Diligence (share only when investor is serious)

| Document | Purpose | Status |
|----------|---------|--------|
| **Company Registration Documents** | Certificate of Incorporation, MoA, AoA | After company registration |
| **IP Ownership / Code Repository** | Proof that you own the IP and codebase | GitHub repo with your ownership |
| **Technical Architecture Document** | Detailed system design, database schema, security | Prepare this |
| **Background Check Docs** | Founder IDs, educational/professional credentials | Collect these |
| **Reference List** | 3-5 people investors can call to verify your claims | Prepare this |
| **Term Sheet Draft** | Your preferred deal terms (equity %, board seats, rights) | Prepare with a lawyer |

### How to Prepare Each Document

#### The One-Pager
Single page, must answer: problem, solution, traction, market size, team, ask. No more than 400 words. Use the Zappy brand colors. Send this via email — investors read it in 60 seconds and decide if they want to meet.

#### The Financial Model
Use Excel or Google Sheets. Must include:
- Monthly revenue projections (Month 1-36)
- Revenue breakdown by stream (subscriptions, commissions, ads)
- Cost breakdown (people, infra, marketing, ops)
- Cash flow statement
- Key assumptions clearly stated
- Break-even analysis

Investors will stress-test your assumptions. Know the bear/base/bull case.

#### The Cap Table
A spreadsheet showing who owns what percentage of the company:
- Founders (you): usually 80-90% pre-seed
- ESOP pool: 10-15% (investors will ask for this)
- Previous investors (if any): list here
- Post-investment: show diluted % after this round

#### Product Demo Video
Record a 3-5 minute Loom video walking through:
1. Customer scanning QR and placing order
2. Kitchen display receiving the order live
3. Waiter dashboard seeing the table update
4. Billing counter generating the invoice
5. Admin seeing the revenue dashboard
6. Super Admin managing all tenants

This is enormously powerful — most investors have never seen a restaurant OS work in real-time.

### Cold Outreach Email Template

Subject: **Zappy — Restaurant OS | MVP Live | ₹2Cr Seed | 7.5M restaurant opportunity**

[Your Name]
[Phone]
zappyscan@gmail.com

---

**Key rules for cold outreach:**
- Keep it under 150 words
- Lead with the problem and market size, not the product
- Always include a live link
- End with a specific, low-commitment ask (20 minutes, not "would you invest?")
- Follow up exactly once after 5 business days if no reply
- Don't send to 50 people at once — personalize each one

### Warm Introductions (10x More Effective)

A warm intro converts 5-10x better than cold outreach. To get intros:

1. Map out your network — who do you know who knows investors?
2. Look at investor portfolio companies — founders of those companies can introduce you
3. LinkedIn: see mutual connections between you and the investor's profile
4. Accelerators and startup events — meet investors in person first
5. Ask your lawyer, CA, or banker — they often know investors

When asking for an intro, send your contact a "forwardable email" — a 5-line summary they can forward directly to the investor without editing.

### Accelerator Programs (Apply in Parallel)

Apply to accelerators even if you're fundraising directly. Accelerators provide:
- ₹15-25L funding
- Mentorship and founder community
- Direct introductions to seed funds at Demo Day
- Credibility signal for future investors

**Recommended accelerators for Zappy:**
- **Axilor Ventures** (Bengaluru) — 10-week program, ₹25L for 4-6% equity
- **NASSCOM 10,000 Startups** — Indian tech ecosystem, strong network
- **Google for Startups India** — cloud credits + investor access
- **Microsoft for Startups** — Azure credits + investor network
- **T-Hub** (Hyderabad) — strong South India restaurant/food-tech ecosystem
- **Startup Tamil Nadu** — government support, local network

## STAGE 5: The Investor Meeting

### Before the Meeting

- Send pitch deck 24 hours before (not earlier — they won't remember it)
- Prepare your demo — test the live product, have a backup screen recording
- Research the investor: their portfolio, recent investments, what they've said publicly
- Prepare answers to the 10 questions below

### The 10 Questions Every Investor Will Ask

Be ready to answer these in every single meeting:

1. **"Tell me about yourself — why are you the right person to build this?"**
   Answer: Your background, what makes you qualified, why you're obsessed with this problem.

2. **"How big is the market?"**
   Answer: TAM ₹1,200Cr, SAM ₹120Cr, SOM ₹12Cr by Year 3. India 7.5M restaurants, 9% CAGR.

3. **"Who are your competitors and why will you win?"**
   Answer: Petpooja (₹1,800/mo, no free tier, no AI), Posist (₹3,000/mo, enterprise-only). Zappy wins on: integrated real-time UX, AI, free tier, SaaS multi-tenancy, zero hardware.

4. **"What's your business model? How do you make money?"**
   Answer: SaaS subscriptions (₹999-₹2,999/mo), transaction fees (0.5-1%), platform ads (CPC), setup fees. 82% gross margin target.

5. **"What traction do you have?"**
   Answer: MVP live, 53 features shipped and tested, v2.4.1 production, 4 live tenants. Pre-revenue — raising to get to revenue.

6. **"What will you do with the money?"**
   Answer: Engineering 40%, Sales 30%, Operations 15%, Working Capital 10%, Legal 5%. Goal: 200 paying restaurants, ₹4.8L MRR by Month 18.

7. **"What could go wrong?"**
   Answer: Restaurant adoption risk (mitigated by free tier + white-glove onboarding). Competitor response (mitigated by integrated UX advantage). Being honest here builds credibility.

8. **"How did you get to this valuation?"**
   Answer: ₹2Cr for 10-15% implies ₹13-20Cr post-money valuation. Comparable SaaS companies at this stage with working MVPs raise at 8-15× ARR. Our 18-month target ARR is ₹57.6L.

9. **"Why now? Why is this the right time?"**
   Answer: Post-COVID contactless dining preference, UPI adoption normalizing digital payments, cloud kitchen boom, ONDC driving restaurant digitisation, labor cost inflation making automation ROI-positive.

10. **"Tell me about your team."**
    Answer: Honest, specific. If you're a solo founder, acknowledge it and explain your hiring plan. Investors know solo founder risk — show them you know how to address it.

### During the Meeting

**Structure your 30-minute meeting:**
- Minutes 0-3: Quick intro, your background, why you're building this
- Minutes 3-8: The problem (use the pain stats)
- Minutes 8-18: Live product demo (this is the most important part)
- Minutes 18-25: Market, financials, ask
- Minutes 25-30: Questions

**Show, don't tell.** Live demo beats slides every time. Open the browser, scan the QR code, place an order — watch the kitchen screen update in real time. That 3 seconds of "wow" does more work than 20 slides.

**Be honest about what you don't know.** Investors respect founders who know their limits. "I don't have the answer to that yet, but here's how I'm thinking about it" is better than bluffing.

### After the Meeting

Within 24 hours, send a follow-up email:
- Thank them for their time
- Address any questions you couldn't answer fully
- Share any documents they asked for
- Ask clearly: "What are your next steps? What additional information would be helpful?"

If they say they need to think — give it exactly one week, then follow up once.

## STAGE 6: Managing the Fundraising Process

### The Fundraising Funnel

You need to contact far more investors than you think:

```
100 investor contacts identified
↓ 40% reply or respond to warm intro
40 initial conversations
↓ 50% want a meeting
20 first meetings
↓ 40% want a second meeting / due diligence
8 serious conversations
↓ 25% convert to term sheets
2 term sheets
↓ You pick the best one
1 signed deal
```

**What this means:** Plan to contact 80-100 investors to close a ₹2 Crore round. Start conversations with everyone in parallel — don't do them sequentially.

### Creating Urgency (Ethically)

Investors move faster when they believe others are interested:
- "We've had strong interest from [other fund] and are targeting to close in 6 weeks"
- "We have a restaurant chain interested in signing a paid contract — we want to close the round before that"
- Never fake urgency. Real urgency is far more effective.

### The SAFE vs. Equity Decision

For a ₹2 Crore seed round, you have two structures:

**SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity)**
- No immediate equity transfer — converts at next priced round
- Faster to close, cheaper on legal fees
- Common in pre-revenue seed rounds
- Risk: valuation cap negotiation is critical

**Priced Equity Round**
- Actual shares issued immediately at agreed valuation
- More paperwork, higher legal cost
- Cleaner cap table long-term
- Better if you have strong negotiating position

**Recommendation for Zappy:** Start with SAFE to close faster. Move to priced round at Series A.

## STAGE 7: Due Diligence — What Investors Will Check

When an investor is serious, they'll conduct due diligence. Be prepared for:

### What They Check

| Area | What They'll Ask For | How to Prepare |
|------|---------------------|---------------|
| **Product** | Demo + code review + security audit | Keep codebase clean, document architecture |
| **Legal** | Company registration, IP ownership, contracts | Register as Pvt Ltd, ensure IP is in company name |
| **Financials** | Bank statements, burn rate, historical expenses | Keep clean books from day one |
| **Team** | Background checks, references, LinkedIn verification | Be accurate on everything in your bio |
| **Market** | Your market size claims, competitor analysis | Have sources for every stat you cite |
| **Customers** | Talk to your users or reference customers | Prepare 2-3 restaurant contacts willing to speak |
| **Technical** | Code quality, scalability, security | Prepare technical architecture document |

### How to Pass Due Diligence

1. **Over-prepare** — have every document in a due diligence data room (use Google Drive or Notion)
2. **Be proactive** — share documents before they ask
3. **Be consistent** — numbers in your deck, report, and model must all match exactly
4. **Disclose proactively** — if there's a problem (no customers yet, solo founder, legal issue), say it first. Investors discover everything. Getting caught hiding something kills deals.

## STAGE 8: Term Sheet to Closing

### Understanding a Term Sheet

A term sheet is a non-binding summary of deal terms. Key terms to understand:

| Term | What It Means | Ideal for You |
|------|--------------|--------------|
| **Pre-money valuation** | Company value before investment | ₹13-20Cr (for ₹2Cr at 10-15%) |
| **Post-money valuation** | Pre-money + investment amount | ₹15-22Cr |
| **Equity %** | What investor gets | 10-15% |
| **Liquidation preference** | Who gets paid first in exit | Push for 1× non-participating |
| **Anti-dilution** | Protection if future round is at lower valuation | Negotiate broad-based weighted average |
| **Board seats** | Who sits on the board | At this stage: 3 seats (2 founders, 1 investor) |
| **Pro-rata rights** | Investor's right to invest in future rounds | Standard — accept this |
| **Information rights** | Investor gets quarterly updates | Standard — accept this |

**Non-negotiables for founders:** Maintain >50% ownership post-seed. Don't give up board control. No full ratchet anti-dilution.

### Get a Lawyer

Before signing any term sheet or shareholder agreement, engage a startup lawyer. Cost: ₹50,000 - ₹1,50,000. Worth every rupee. Recommended firms for Indian startups: Trilegal, AZB & Partners, Khaitan & Co, Nishith Desai Associates, iSPIRT legal network for early-stage.

### Closing the Round

After term sheet:
1. Lawyer drafts Shareholder Agreement (SHA) and Share Subscription Agreement (SSA)
2. Board resolution authorizing share issuance
3. RBI filings for foreign investment (if investor is overseas)
4. Money transfers and share issuance
5. Update MCA (Ministry of Corporate Affairs) filings
6. Update cap table

Timeline: 4-8 weeks from term sheet to money in account.

## STAGE 9: After You Close — Investor Relations

Closing the round is not the end — it's the beginning of a relationship.

### What Investors Expect After Closing

- **Monthly updates** (2-page email): MRR, burn rate, key wins, key challenges, next month targets
- **Quarterly board meetings** (if board seats given)
- **Immediate communication** if something goes wrong — don't hide bad news
- **Access and introductions** — ask your investors to open doors, that's why they're there

### Monthly Investor Update Template

```

Highlights:
• MRR: ₹X (target was ₹Y) — [up/down] X%
• New restaurants signed: X (cumulative: X)

Key wins this month:
1. [specific win]

Key challenges:

1. [goal]
```

Investors who get honest monthly updates become your biggest advocates. They refer you to their network, help you hire, and follow on in the next round.

## Quick Reference: The Investor Journey Timeline

| Week | Action |
|------|--------|
| **Week 1-2** | Finalize all documents (deck, report, financial model, one-pager, demo video) |
| **Week 3** | List 100 target investors; segment into warm (have intro), lukewarm (mutual connection), cold |
| **Week 4** | Begin outreach — warm intros first, cold emails second |
| **Week 5-8** | First meetings. Aim for 20+ first meetings. Refine pitch based on feedback |
| **Week 9-12** | Second meetings and due diligence with serious investors |
| **Week 13-16** | Term sheet negotiations, legal, closing |
| **Week 17-18** | Money in account. Team building begins immediately |

**Total timeline: 4-5 months from start to close** (typical for Indian seed rounds). Plan accordingly and don't run out of money before you close.

## Key Principles to Remember

1. **Fundraising is a sales process.** You're selling equity. Treat it like sales — pipeline, follow-ups, close rates.

2. **The best fundraising story is revenue.** Every rupee of MRR you have before raising makes the next rupee of investment easier to get.

3. **Investors invest in founders first, market second, product third.** Show conviction, coachability, and self-awareness.

4. **Never take money from someone you wouldn't want to call at 11pm with bad news.** You'll be working with them for 7-10 years.

5. **Rejection is information.** When investors say no, ask why. Common patterns in rejections tell you what to fix.

6. **Momentum is everything.** Once you have one yes, others follow quickly. Create a 6-week deadline and stick to it.

7. **Parallel process everything.** Never wait for one investor to say no before talking to the next. Run 10-20 conversations simultaneously.

8. **Your product is your best pitch.** A live, working product that solves a real problem beats any deck. With Zappy, the 10-second demo (scan QR → kitchen updates in real-time) is your most powerful closing tool.
